Filipino Pork Adobo

Adobo, a cooking style originating from the Philippines, involves simmering meat in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic. The result is a tangy, savory dish that made it to one of the Filipinos' all time favorites!

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 pounds pork shoulder(cut into chunks)
  • 1/4 cup cane vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/3 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 6 cloves garlic(chopped)
  • 1 bay leap
  • 2 teaspoons black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 cups water

Directions:

  1. In a medium dutch oven or pot over medium heat, add the oil and sear the pork until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaf, peppercorns (tied in cheese cloth if desired), sugar, and water, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Remove the cover and continue simmering for another 30 minutes to reduce the sauce.
  4. Serve over rice.
